Step 4 / Making Pallette

PCB Ribbon

You will need:

  • Assembled PCBs
  • Ribbon cable
  • Energizer 376 cell batteries
  • Soldering iron
  • Solder wire
  • Wire cutter
  • Multimeter
  • CC Debugger
  • Programming cable
  • Tape
  • Marker

Now you should have boards that look like this:

  1. The Battery Board

  2. The Voltage Regulator Board

  3. The Sensor Board

  4. The Microcontroller Board

Or alternatively this (photo features older design):

Trim the legs

You need to trim the legs of the connectors with a cutter.


Now we have the little PCBs with components soldered on them, we need to connect them together to make a complete circuitry. However, before we do that, it's better to test each board to make sure that there are no soldering mistakes.

Test the Battery Board

You need 2x batteries and a multimeter.

Stack two batteries and tape their side together.

Push the batteries inside the battery holder, positive side (flat side) up.

If the batteries are too loose in the battery holder, add a bit of solder to the central round pad of the battery holder footprint.

Turn on the switch by flipping the lever up, and measure the voltage across the 2 pin header, the reading should be around 3.1V.

Test the Voltage Regulator Board

Plug in the battery board to the voltage regulator board, and turn it on.

Measure the output voltage from the regulator using the pads at the back of the board. The multimeter should read 2.2V.

To test the Sensor Board and the Microcontroller Board, we need to connect them with ribbon cables. The next section shows you how.

With the clay imprint and the prepared PCB's, we're ready to craft the heart of Pallette - the sensor ribbon. You'll align the boards against the clay imprint, measure lengths and angles for ribbon cables, and solder everything together.

Connecting boards

Tape boards at desired locations

We will use 0.05" pitch ribbon cables to connect them. Cut two 5cm 2-conductor ribbon cables and a 5cm 6-conductor ribbon cable.

Strip the ends of the ribbon cables.

Tint the ends with solder.

Solder the conductors on the pads at the back of the Voltage Regulator Board and the Microcontroller Board, and tape them back on the clay imprint.

Align the Sensor Board, and mark the board edge with a pen.

Mark the lengths of the ribbon cables using the pads on the back on the Sensor Board.

Cut the ribbon cables to the desired lengths, strip the ends, and solder the conductors to the corresponding pads.

Now we have the assembled sensor band.

We have to test the assembled Pallette sensor band to ensure that the circuit functions correctly before we seal it up. The next section explains how to use the firmware and the app to test the circuit.
