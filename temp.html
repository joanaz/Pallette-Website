Electronics

You will need:

  • Electronics design files
  • Zinc air batteries
  • Ribbon cable 6 conductors 0.05" pitch
  • Soldering iron
  • Solder wire
  • Wire stripper
  • Wire cutter

If you want to do reflow soldering youself, you will need

  • Electronic hot plate preheating station or hot air soldering rework station
  • Solder paste
  • Solder wick
  • Flux
  • Tweezers
  • Components listed on the BOM

The schematics and the PCB are designed using Altium ® Designer, a professional PCB design software for the Microsoft Windows environment. However, you don't need to obtain a copy of Altium ® Designer if you don't wish to modify the design.

All the design files, gerber files and docs are available on this Github repo. You can just send the Fabrication.zip on the repo to a PCB manufacturer to receive the Pallette PCBs. Here you can either use the specs list on the design files, or choose your own specs. If you were to use our specs, you will get white 0.014" thin 370HR boards with gold immersion finish and v-scores, which will be quite pricey (>$100 each for 10).

Or you can get them for $33 each with this special deal. However the boards will be 4x thicker (0.062") with lead free solder finish, and you'll have to cut the boards yourself.

After you have received the PCBs from the manufacturer, you can either purchase the components as listed on the Bill of Materials (BOM) in the repo and solder the components yourself, or send your blank PCBs and the Assembly.zip to a PCB assembler to receive assembled PCBs. Firmware

You will need:

  • Firmware files
  • Programming cable
  • CC Debugger
  • CC Debugger driver
  • Bluegiga SDK
  • Windows environment

The firmware runs on the Bluetooth enabled BLE113 microcontroller. The files are on this Github repo.

To program the microcontroller, you will need this cable.

and CC Debugger

You will need to install the CC Debugger driver, and the Bluegiga SDK on the Windows environment.
