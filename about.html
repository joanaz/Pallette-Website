What is Pallette?

Pallette is the world's first open source tongue-computer interface. A wireless, universal remote control for the tongue, worn like a mouthguard.

How it works.

Pallette uses infared proximity sensors to track the tip of the tongue, and a microphone to listen for teeth "clicks". Moving your tongue controls things and clicking your teeth together makes decisions. The device can connect to a tablet or computer directly as a bluetooth mouse, or can be connected to a custom Android or iPhone app, enabling a world's worth of unique tongue-technology interactions.

The Dream

We've designed and opensourced Pallette the goals of providing greater independence to the mobility impaired, and making tongue-based interfaces publically accepted and a prominant method of assistive, creative interaction.

Tongue-based control is private (no voice commands), discrete (hidden from view), and affordable (inexpensive hardware). It is our hope that Pallette will create avenues of creative exploration and meaningful assistance.
