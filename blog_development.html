Further Develop Pallette

Pallette can be expanded on multiple fronts. Hardware, Software, Design. Or something else - organically. Here we detail our suggested directions and how to push them.

Hardware

Currently the Energizer 376 high drain batteries can last around 2 hours. A longer continuous use of Pallette will require batteries with higher capacities but comparable size, so they can comfortably fit inside the mouth.

Alternatively, it would be interesting to investigate piezoelectric generators, so batteries could be recharged from mouth motions like speaking or chewing anytime. An additional benefit would be that since then batteries wouldn't need to be changed, the circuitry could be sealed completely, so the Nylon tubing and silicon stopper would be no longer required, result in easier assembly and smaller form.

Other ideas with similar benefits are energy harvesting from radio waves, and inductive charging. However, no matter which idea to pursue, it would be limited by the space inside the mouth and the electrical capacity requirement of the Pallette sensor band. Therefore, another important path to explore is how to maintain or even improve the tongue control accuracy with less power consumption.

Interaction technique wise, we have explored joystick, physical buttons, force sensing resistors. Now we have decided on infrared sensors and a microphone. It would be cool to see the works of other interaction strategy used inside the mouth, such as projected capacitive touch or force sensitive touch.

Software

The ultimate goal of Pallette is for it to control all technologies important to a user. We envisioned that with the booming of internet-of-things, mobile phones would become more enabled at controlling surrounding technologies. Therefore we made Pallette to control the phone like a mouse cursor. However many current assistive technologies cannot be controlled by the phone or are not connected to the internet. We'd love to see developers help include more assistive technologies to the internet-of-things space, or build applications that allow Pallette to be more helpful to assistive technologies.

Design

We chose off-the-shelf mouthguard as part of the form, so it'd be easier to build Pallette from scrach, though we have explored orthodontic retainers and we believe that they would present a better form when the Pallette circuitry was integrated within. Since orthodonic retainers are custom made, they are thinner and sleeker, and fit better inside the mouth than a mouthguard. Please let us know if you figure out a good way to seal Pallette circuitry inside a retainer.
